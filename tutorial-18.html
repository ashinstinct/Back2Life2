Sea Colossus
You vs an Ocean Titan

Turn a single selfie into a flying superhero battling a skyscraper-sized water titan across a storm ocean — a full five-shot cinematic sequence generated from one structured Seedance 2.0 prompt and three reference images.

Tool: Seedance 2.0
Shots: 5-shot sequence
Difficulty: Advanced
Time: ~25 mins
Follow Instagram TikTok YouTube

What You'll Create

This is the most structured prompt format in the series — a full reference-locked, five-shot action sequence written like a mini screenplay. You supply three reference images: your superhero portrait (@Image1), a character board that locks your suit and proportions (@Image2), and a sea monster design board (@Image3). The prompt then locks both characters so they stay identical across all five shots — approach, engagement, charge, strike, and aftermath — while the model handles the ocean physics, storm atmosphere, and final vortex explosion.

▸A five-shot superhero vs kaiju battle sequence over a storm ocean
▸Your own face locked onto the hero via @Image1 + a character board
▸A translucent water titan with a rotating vortex core that stays consistent
▸A full negative-prompt block that kills flicker, duplicates, and anime drift
▸A reusable reference-lock structure for any hero-vs-monster concept

1

Create Your Three Reference Images

The reference lock only works if the images exist first

1

Start with a clear selfie or portrait — good light, face straight to camera. Use an image model (GPT Image 2, or the Back2Life AI Studio) to turn it into a full-body superhero portrait: dark tactical suit with glowing blue detailing works best against the storm ocean palette.

2

Generate a character board from that portrait — front, side and back views plus face close-ups on one sheet. This is @Image2 and it is what keeps your face and suit identical across all five shots.

3

Generate a sea monster design board — a massive humanoid water titan with a translucent ocean body, rotating vortex core, and a whirlpool lower body, shown at multiple angles with scale references. This is @Image3.

4

Keep all three images in 16:9 or square, high resolution, with clean uncluttered backgrounds.

💡

Pro tip: The character board matters more than the portrait. If the model ever loses your face mid-sequence, it is almost always because the board lacked a clear front-facing close-up.

Paste The Prompt & Generate

Copy it exactly — then customise once you've seen a clean run

1

Upload your three reference images in order — portrait, character board, monster board — so they map to @Image1, @Image2 and @Image3.

2

Copy the full structured prompt below and paste it in. Yes, all of it — the negative prompt block at the end is doing a lot of the heavy lifting.

3

Generate. If a shot drifts (face changes, monster mutates), regenerate — the reference lock keeps most runs consistent but Seedance still varies between seeds.

📋 Prompt
reference_lock:
  main_character:
    source: "@Image1"
    character board: "@Image2"
    context: "face reference"
    instructions:
    - preserve exact facial identity
    - preserve hairstyle and facial structure
    - maintain consistent outfit silhouette
    - maintain body proportions across all shots
    - single character only
    - no duplicates
    - realistic human anatomy

  sea_monster:
    source: "@Image3"
    context: "sea monster reference"
    instructions:
    - preserve creature anatomy and silhouette
    - maintain consistent scale across all shots
    - preserve translucent ocean-water body structure
    - maintain consistent vortex core design
    - no mutation or redesign
    - realistic movement weight

visual_style:
