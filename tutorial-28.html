Character Sheets
The Master Turnaround Prompt

One master prompt that turns your reference photos into a premium film-studio character turnaround board — hero face close-up, face rotation grid, T-poses and a full body turnaround on a 9:16 canvas. This sheet is the backbone of character consistency in every video tutorial on this site.

Tool: AI Image Model
Output: 9:16 sheet
Difficulty: Beginner
Time: ~10 mins
Follow Instagram TikTok YouTube

What You'll Create

A character sheet is the single highest-leverage asset in AI video. Video models don't remember your character between generations — the sheet does it for them. This master prompt builds a studio-grade turnaround board with a strict hierarchy: a large hero face close-up and rotation grid on top (identity first), then T-poses and a full body turnaround below (proportions and outfit second). Feed the finished sheet into Seedance as a character board reference and your face, outfit and proportions survive every shot, every cut, every regeneration.

Gather Your Reference Photos

The sheet is only as good as what goes in

1

Collect 2–4 photos of your character: at minimum one sharp front-facing portrait, plus a 3/4 angle and a full-body shot if you have them.

2

Even, natural lighting and a neutral expression work best — dramatic shadows in the reference become inconsistencies across panels.

3

If your character already exists as an AI generation (like a saved Higgsfield element), use its cleanest, most canonical renders as the references.

4

Decide the outfit before generating — a character sheet locks one look. Different outfit = different sheet (that's what the clothing prompts below are for).

💡

Pro tip: Make one sheet per outfit, not one sheet per character. The whole point is that every panel shows identical clothing — a "greatest hits" sheet with mixed outfits teaches the video model to randomise your wardrobe.

Paste The Prompt & Generate

Copy it exactly — then customise once you've seen a clean run

1

Upload your reference photos to your image model of choice.

2

Copy the master prompt below and paste it as-is.

3

Append one clothing prompt from the gallery further down (or write your own in the same style) to the end of the master prompt.

4

Generate, then zoom in and audit: same face in every panel, same outfit details panel to panel, clean gutters, no stray text. Regenerate until the sheet passes — a flawed sheet poisons every video made from it.

📋 Character Sheet Master Prompt
Character reference sheet — enlarged hero face close-up, face rotation grid, T-pose front, T-pose back, Front neutral, Side — left, Side — right, 3/4 front, 3/4 back, Full back, Walking pose.

Layout: vertical 9:16 portrait canvas, designed like a premium film/game studio character turnaround board.

Top section (identity focus): one very large hero face close-up spanning ~50–60% of the width, positioned prominently as the main visual anchor. Beside it: a compact face rotation sheet/grid showing multiple head angles (front, 3/4, side, back, looking down/up) plus one supporting portrait crop. This top row should immediately establish facial identity.

Middle section: T-pose front, T-pose back, Front neutral (3 evenly spaced panels).

Lower section: Side — left, Side — right, 3/4 front, Full back, 3/4 back, Walking pose arranged in clean stacked rows with balanced spacing.

Full-body panels remain large enough to preserve proportions and outfit detail while prioritizing the face sheet at the top.

Composition must fully utilize the tall 9:16 frame, with a strong hierarchy: face identity first, body turnaround second.

Match the visual style, face, outfit, skin texture, hair, and proportions from the reference images exactly. No redesign, no stylization, no reinterpretation. Photorealistic appearance preserved 100% consistently across every panel.

Neutral grey studio backdrop. Soft studio three-point lighting, flat even illumination, no harsh shadows. Thin white gutters separating panels. No text, no labels, no watermarks.
🎬

The line "No redesign, no stylization, no reinterpretation" is the guard rail — image models love to "improve" faces. If panels still drift, add "identical facial features in all panels, same person throughout" at the end.

Outfit Prompts

Append one of these to the master prompt — one sheet per outfit

Each clothing prompt below builds a different sheet of the same character. Generate one per look your character needs, and note how every prompt ends by pinning details "identical across all panels" — that phrase is what stops outfit drift between panels.
