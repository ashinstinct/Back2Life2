The Jump
Helicopter Chase & Truck-Ramp Escape

A timestamped 15-second action sequence: you sprint through gunfire, jump on a combat motorcycle, launch off a flatbed truck ramp, and shoot down the pursuing helicopter mid-air — all in one unbroken tracking shot.

Tool: Seedance 2.0
Shot: One 15s take
Difficulty: Beginner
Time: ~10 mins
Follow Instagram TikTok YouTube

What You'll Create

This prompt shows off timestamped pacing — five timed beats (0-3s, 3-5s, 5-9s, 9-12s, 12-15s) inside one continuous camera move. The timestamps aren't cuts; they tell the model exactly when each beat happens so the sprint, the bike mount, the chase, the ramp jump and the tunnel exit all land on schedule instead of the model rushing or stalling. It's the single most useful technique to steal for any action prompt.

Prepare Your Reference Photo

Match the wardrobe the prompt expects

1

The prompt puts the character in plain dark civilian clothes — a photo of you in a dark t-shirt or hoodie gives the cleanest identity match.

2

A full-body or half-body shot helps here more than a face-only selfie, because the character sprints and rides — the model needs your build, not just your face.

3

Face clearly visible, no sunglasses — the prompt specifically keeps your face visible until the helmet goes on at the 3-5s beat.

💡

Pro tip: Note how the prompt insists on ONE single helicopter, in caps, twice. Repetition + capitalisation is how you stop Seedance spawning a second helicopter halfway through the chase.

📋 Prompt
Single continuous uninterrupted cinematic shot, no cuts, one flowing 15-second take. Photorealistic live-action film, NOT animated, NOT a video game, NOT CGI render. Desaturated cool palette with warm amber accents, deep shadow falloff, fine 35mm grain, high-budget professional action film look. Bright midday daylight, harsh sun, urban streets.

The main character [IMAGE REFERENCE] wears plain dark civilian clothes.

0-3s: extremely fast tracking shot following the character sprinting at full speed toward a parked motorcycle, his face clearly visible in tense expression — from the very first frame, ONE single military helicopter is already chasing him low from behind and actively FIRING at him, bullets striking the ground and walls around him as he runs, dust and debris kicking up, and he dodges left and right, ducking and weaving between the incoming shots.

3-5s: still in the same unbroken shot, he reaches the motorcycle — a heavy combat motorcycle with a mounted machine gun fixed on its side — grabs the helmet, puts it on quickly (face fully visible until the helmet goes on), and accelerates hard onto the main road.

5-9s: the SAME single helicopter keeps pursuing low and firing, its shots barely missing as the rider swerves through traffic with sharp aggressive maneuvers, sparks and impacts erupting around the speeding motorcycle.

9-12s: ahead on the road stands a heavy flatbed transport truck parked at an angle, its long cargo bed forming a steep upward ramp with side rails — the rear loading end low to the ground and the front end raised high — the rider races straight up the angled truck bed at full speed and launches high off the raised end into the air; while mid-air, he activates the side-mounted machine gun and fires a sustained burst at the pursuing helicopter, and the helicopter erupts in a massive violent explosion, fireball and debris raining across the bright daytime sky.

12-15s: after landing, the rider continues at full speed and disappears into a long dark underpass tunnel, the camera still tracking aggressively from behind. Camera stays low, fast and reactive the entire time, like real documentary action footage. No music.
🎬

If you change the clip length, rescale the timestamps proportionally — a 10-second version needs 0-2s / 2-3.5s / 3.5-6s / 6-8s / 8-10s beats, not the original numbers.
